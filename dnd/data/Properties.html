Standart Properties

Ammunition

You can use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition to fire from the weapon. Each time you attack with the weapon, you expend one piece of ammunition. Drawing the ammunition from a quiver, case, or other container is part of the attack (you need a free hand to load a one-handed weapon). At the end of the battle, you can recover half your expended ammunition by taking a minute to search the battlefield.

If you use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a melee attack, you treat the weapon as an improvised weapon (see "Improvised Weapons" later in the section). A sling must be loaded to deal any damage when used in this way.

Finesse

When making an attack with a finesse weapon, you use your choice of your Strength or Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both rolls.

Heavy

Creatures that are Small or Tiny have disadvantage on attack rolls with heavy weapons. A heavy weapon's size and bulk make it too large for a Small or Tiny creature to use effectively.

Light

A light weapon is small and easy to handle, making it ideal for use when fighting with two weapons.

Loading

Because of the time required to load this weapon, you can fire only one piece of ammunition from it when you use an action, bonus action, or reaction to fire it, regardless of the number of attacks you can normally make.

Range

A weapon that can be used to make a ranged attack has a range shown in parentheses after the ammunition or thrown property. The range lists two numbers. The first is the weapon's normal range in feet, and the second indicates the weapon's long range. When attacking a target beyond normal range, you have disadvantage on the attack roll. You can't attack a target beyond the weapon's long range.

Reach

This weapon adds 5 feet to your reach when you attack with it, as well as when determining your reach for opportunity attacks with it.

Special

A weapon with the special property has unusual rules governing its use, explained in the weapon's description (see "Special Weapons" later in this section).

Thrown

If a weapon has the thrown property, you can throw the weapon to make a ranged attack. If the weapon is a melee weapon, you use the same ability modifier for that attack roll and damage roll that you would use for a melee attack with the weapon. For example, if you throw a handaxe, you use your Strength, but if you throw a dagger, you can use either your Strength or your Dexterity, since the dagger has the finesse property.

Two-Handed

This weapon requires two hands when you attack with it.

Versatile

This weapon can be used with one or two hands. A damage value in parentheses appears with the property--the damage when the weapon is used with two hands to make a melee attack.

New Properties

Reload(X)

When wielding a weapons with a magazin (or something similar), you have to reload the weapon after X shots.

Reloading takes the entire bonus action and an free hand is required.

Movement-Loading(X)

When wielding a weapon with this property, it has to be loaded after every shot but it is so easy to load, that you can easily do that by sacrificing some mobility.

When loading one shot in a round, you can move X feat less this round. If you don't have movement left this round, your weapon stays unloaded. You can use your bonus action to load the weapon.

Loading requires an free hand.

Armor-Piercing(X)

Armor piercing weapons permantenly damage the armor of the target.

  • X = -1 and AC >= 14 -> -1 AC
  • X = -2 and AC >= 16 -> -2 AC
  • X = -3 and AC >= 18 -> -3 AC
  • X = -4 and AC >= 20 -> -4 AC
  • X = -5 and AC >= 22 -> -5 AC

(AC refers the targets AC.)

This effect stacks, but is based on the current AC of the target, not the original AC.

Short-Burst(X)

Some weapons are automatic or use burst fire, so you shoot multiple rounds of ammo when pulling the trigger. This is optional.

1+X bullets are shot at once.

When rolling for damage, you can reroll X die.

(F.e. 3D6 -> 1,2,4. You have and use Short-Burst(2) -> Reroll 2 die (logically the lowest ones make sense).)

Gun

When a weapon is a gun, the attack roll is made against BAC.

Range(X/Y/Z)

Like Range(Y/Z), but disadvantage when target is within X feat. X is Y/4.

Suppressed

The gun shot sound is suppressed and the gun is way more silent.

Scope(X)

A scope can be attached to almost every gun. Then a scope is attacked, every Range property attribute is multiplied by X. If Range(X/Y) is the current Range, it is changed to Range(X/Y/Z).

When using a weapon with a scope, you can't move in the round you fire.

Deattaching a scope in combat takes 2 whole rounds. Attaching a scope in combat takes 2 whole rounds and the attack rolls with this weapon for the next 4 rounds are with disadvantage (readjusting the scope).

Weapons

Price = ( ( ( MaxDMG + MinDMG + ( FlatDMG * 2 ) )^( 2.5 ) ) / 2.5 ) * ( 1 )
  • MaxDMG is the max damage, a weapon can deal in one round.
  • MinDMG is the minimal damage, a weapon can deal in one round (or the amount of dice, that are rolled).

F.e.: 2D12 -> 24+2,; 4D6 -> 24+4; 6D4 -> 24+6.

  • FlatDMG is f.e., if a weapon does 1D6+2 +StrMod or +DexMod. Meaning, the +2 is always there, no matter the modifier. The min and max damage are thereby both increased by 2, thus FlatDMG*2.
Elemental Damage:

For damage types, that are not bludgeoning, piercing or slashing, the percentage of the price is double.

F.e.: 1D6 bludgeoning + 1D6 fire

-> Lets assume for simplisity, the price ist 250 Credits. 50% of the damage is fire, that means 125 Credits of the price are for the fire damage. Double that

-> 125 Credits bludgeoning + (125 Credits fire)*2 = 375 Credits

For special effects, decide individually.

Armor

Price = ( e / 1.8 )^( AC )

  • Effects like +1 Stealth are calculated by adding the effect value to the AC.

  • Disadvantage on Stealth can reduce the price between 10%.

  • If the armor is supposed to have 0 AC, a base AC of 10 is used.

  • Armor with 15-19 AC have BAC +1; Armor with 20+ AC have BAC +2 (Unless specified differently).

Armor Types:
  • Light:
    • AC +Dex (Inf)
    • Price -> AC +2
  • Medium:
    • AC +Dex (max 2)
    • Price -> AC +1
  • Heavy:
    • AC +0
    • Price -> AC

For special effects, decide individually.

BAC (Bullet Armor Class)

Calculation

It's calculated like normal AC.

Traits like Unarmored Defense (Barbarien) work with BAC.

When is it used?

If you get attacked by a weapon with the Gun-Property, the BAC is used instead of the standard AC.

tl;dr: When you get shot.

Cost

The price is as high as for normal AC.
